Filipino Chicken Adobo

Filipino Chicken Adobo is a beloved comfort food known for its rich, savory taste and mouthwatering aroma. The chicken is gently simmered in a mix of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black pepper, creating a balance of salty, tangy, and slightly bold flavors. As it cooks, the sauce thickens and coats the chicken, making every bite tender, juicy, and deeply satisfying. It is simple in ingredients but full of character, proving that great flavor does not need to be complicated.

More than just a dish, Chicken Adobo holds a special place in Filipino culture and family life. It is often served at home gatherings, celebrations, and everyday meals, bringing people together around the table. Each family has its own version, passed down through generations, making adobo a personal and shared tradition at the same time. Loved by locals and visitors alike, Filipino Chicken Adobo offers a warm taste of the Philippines in every serving.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken
  • 3 pieces dried bay leaves
  • 4 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 6 tablespoons white vinegar
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon whole peppercorn

Steps

  1. Combine chicken, soy sauce, and garlic in a large bowl. Mix well. Marinate the chicken for at least 1 hour. Note: the longer the time, the better
  2. Heat a cooking pot. Pour cooking oil.
  3. When the oil is hot enough, pan-fry the marinated chicken for 2 minutes per side.
  4. Pour-in the remaining marinade, including garlic. Add water. Bring to a boil.
  5. Add dried bay leaves and whole peppercorn. Simmer for 30 minutes or until the chicken gets tender.
  6. Add vinegar. Stir and cook for 10 minutes.
  7. Put-in the sugar, and salt. Stir and turn the heat off.Serve hot. Share and Enjoy!
